EyeOnWater Sketchplots

Xavier Ho | status: draft | updated 2017.11.05

This is a series of data sketches and plotting explorations, which I call sketchplots. The goal is to dive deep into EyeOnWater colour data and visualise interesting trends. EyeOnWater is a citizen science intitiative with an app that supports water colour measurement anyone can do on a smartphone. Data is open access.

I downloaded the entire historical colour dataset, which ranges from 1930 - 2013. The colour measurements were probably taken during voyages across the sea. They are indexed by Forel-Ule.

The Forel-Ule Index

Each colour is mapped to an index number

The Forel-Ule index is a rough but useful indicator for the water type. While there are many factors that may result in the same colour, we can use it as a baseline the compare nonetheless, and it is easy to measure.

If we group nearby measurements and display the most recent measurements, it look like this on the world map. Hover over each hexagon to see the data point.

Historical water colours collected between 1930 - 2013

The plot is drawn in chronological order, from the earliest to the latest.

We can plot the data points over time to see the frequency of colours over time.

Distribution of Forel-Ule colour measured over time

The distribution of colours is largely found in the blue-aqua colour, and there was a significant amount of data collected between 1970 and 2000.
